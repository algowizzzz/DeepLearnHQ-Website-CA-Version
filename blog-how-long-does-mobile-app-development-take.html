How Long Does Mobile App Development Actually Take?

Kinza Tahir·Dec 18, 2025·5 min read

Every agency promises a 6-week mobile app. Real timelines are different. Here's what mobile app development actually takes — phase by phase.

"We can build your app in 6 weeks." This is one of the most common lies in software development. Sometimes it's a genuine misunderstanding of scope. Sometimes it's just telling you what you want to hear.

Here's what mobile app development actually takes — honestly.

Phase 1: Discovery and Design (2–4 weeks)

Before a single line of code is written, the team needs to understand what they're building. This includes: requirements gathering and documentation, user flow mapping, wireframes and UI mockups, technical architecture decisions, and API design.

Cutting this phase short is the single most common cause of project delays and cost overruns. Teams that skip discovery spend the saved time (plus interest) on rework during development.

Phase 2: Development (8–16 weeks, varies by complexity)

Development timelines by app type:

  • Simple app (3–5 screens, basic functionality, no backend): 4–8 weeks
  • Standard app (10–20 screens, custom backend, third-party integrations): 10–16 weeks
  • Complex app (20+ screens, real-time features, complex business logic, AI): 16–28 weeks

These timelines assume a team of 2–3 engineers working full-time. Part-time teams or teams working across multiple projects take proportionally longer.

Phase 3: Testing and QA (2–4 weeks)

Quality assurance is not optional and not instantaneous. A properly tested mobile app goes through: unit testing during development, integration testing, device compatibility testing (20+ device/OS combinations), performance testing, and security testing.

Apps that skip QA have a characteristic pattern: decent App Store reviews for a week, followed by one-star reviews about crashes and data loss.

Phase 4: App Store Submission (1–2 weeks)

Apple's App Store review takes 1–7 days (average: 1–2 days, but rejections require resubmission). Google Play review: 3–7 days. Budget this time — launches that miss a specific date because of App Store review are embarrassing and preventable.

Realistic Total Timelines

  • Simple MVP: 10–14 weeks
  • Standard app: 16–24 weeks
  • Complex app: 24–40 weeks
