How to prompt ChatGPT to get the best results

(A top "People also ask" — read this first; it makes every prompt below better.) One habit beats any prompt list: structure your request as Role + Task + Context + Format, then iterate. In our own test, "Write about marketing" produced generic filler, while the structured version produced a publishable post — same model, same cost. The prompts below come pre-structured, but the real superpower is writing your own this way. (Full method: prompt engineering.)

The best ChatGPT prompts, ranked by impact

🥇 The "ask me questions first" prompt (highest ROI)

"I want to [goal]. Before you answer, ask me the 5 most important questions you need answered to do this really well. Then wait for my responses."

Why it's #1: it flips ChatGPT from guessing to understanding your specifics. Works for writing, planning, decisions — almost anything. This single prompt improves every other output.

🥈 The reusable "expert reviewer"

"Act as an experienced [role]. Review this [thing] and give me: 3 things that work, 3 weaknesses, and the single most important fix. Be direct."

Why: turns ChatGPT into a candid critic. Great for emails, resumes, pitches, code, essays.

🥉 The "explain then simplify"

"Explain [topic] thoroughly, then give me a one-paragraph version I could tell a 12-year-old."

Why: you get both depth and a version you can actually use or share.

Best ChatGPT prompts for writing

(A top related search — see also ChatGPT for writing.)

  • "Rewrite this in a [tone] for [audience], keeping it under [X] words: [paste]."
  • "Give me 3 versions of this: one safe, one bold, one funny: [paste]."
  • "Act as a tough editor — cut every unnecessary word and show the tighter version: [paste]."

Best ChatGPT prompts for self-improvement

(A top related search.)

  • "Act as a thoughtful coach. Ask me about my goal for [area], then help me build a realistic weekly plan."
  • "Help me reflect: here's what happened this week [paste]. Ask me 3 questions to draw out the lesson."
  • "I keep struggling with [habit]. Give me 3 small, specific changes — not generic advice."

"Crazy" or viral prompts — the honest take

(PAAs like "crazy ChatGPT prompts" / "coolest thing you can do with ChatGPT.") The genuinely impressive stuff isn't a secret prompt — it's ChatGPT doing your real work: drafting a business plan, debugging code, turning a spreadsheet into insights, planning a trip end-to-end. The viral "crazy" prompts are usually novelty (role-play, weird personas) that wear off fast. The prompts that keep impressing you are the ones that save you an hour. Chase those.

What people actually recommend (the Reddit reality)

(A top related search: "best chatgpt prompts reddit.") Community favorites skew toward practical, reusable prompts — the "ask me questions first" pattern, expert-reviewer prompts, and tone-rewrites — and away from copy-paste "mega-prompt" lists that look impressive but rarely fit your exact task. The lesson: reusable patterns beat one-off magic prompts.

Build your own "best" prompts

The best prompt is the one tailored to your recurring task:

  1. Structure it (Role + Task + Context + Format).
  2. Add "ask me questions first" when the task needs your specifics.
  3. Iterate and save your winners into a personal library.
